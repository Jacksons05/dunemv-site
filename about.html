About

It started with a missing suitcase.

Dune opened on Winter Street in April 2024 with a simple promise: a man should be able to arrive on the island with nothing and leave the shop with everything he actually needs.

That idea — lose the suitcase, rebuild the wardrobe — is the whole brief. Everything on the racks earns its place against it: the fine-knit polo you live in by day, the linen shirt for the heat off the harbor, the trousers that travel, the one good pair of shoes, and the smart layer for the wedding the island always seems to be throwing.

It is a men's shop with a point of view rather than a catalog — contemporary and heritage labels gathered by hand, kept small on purpose, and arranged so browsing feels less like shopping and more like being let into someone's very well-edited closet.

The Founders

Jamie & Annette

Dune is run by Jamie Kiersted and Annette Luna, a husband-and-wife team. Jamie grew up on the Vineyard, spent more than a decade in Los Angeles working in advertising, and came back at the start of the pandemic to the family business — The Boneyard.

The men's shop is the next chapter of that homecoming: a successor in spirit to the island's much-missed Sundog, built by two people who know exactly which island rules are worth keeping and which are worth breaking.

The Room

Masculine, but flamboyant

Two floors on Winter Street: a lower level painted a deep navy, a stairwell lined with vintage concert posters and photographs, and a shark arcing over the doorway. It was renovated to feel like a study you could shop — a room with opinions.
